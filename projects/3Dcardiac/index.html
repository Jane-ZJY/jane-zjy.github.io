Construction of 3D Cardiac Tissue

Layers, aligned CMs; sychronized beating

Overview

This is the current project I am working on. I started this work because of my interest in regenerative medicine and unique physiology of heart. Inspired by Yaobin’s paper “Interwoven Aligned Conductive Nanofiber Yarn/Hydrogel Composite Scaffolds for Engineered 3D Cardiac Anisotropy” published in 2017, I was thinking if the designed multi-layer anisotropic structure of engineered tissue would make a big difference on the beating function or the maturation of cardiomyocytes. Herein, I focus on improving the methods for constructing cardiac tissue models which can individually control the orientation of every layer in a 3D environment, and achieve enough contact of the cells inter-/intra-layer.

I designed three models, and for now, I have been conducting the first one, using layers of suspended aligned nanofiber films seeded with CMs and then encapsulated by hydrogel, to mimic the structure and 3D ECM environment of native cardiac tissue (see phased results). This model can induce cells anisotropic arrangement, maturation and directional beating. The other two models will use cell sheet and bio-printing technology. I hope these engineered tissues will be used in myocardial injury treatment and drug screening, or integrated in organ-on-a-chip in the future.
