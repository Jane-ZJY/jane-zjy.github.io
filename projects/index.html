3D Cardiac Tissue Construction

Aligned CMs, sychronized beating

This is my graudate thesis project at Southeast University. My ultimate task is to construct 3D cardiac tissue models with biomimetic multi-layered anisotropic architecture. Specifically, from the endocardium to the epicardium, the orientation of cardiomyocytes is gradually deflected. Eventually, I have successfully fabricated the 3D model with biomimetic structure——by encapsulating layers of suspended aligned nanofiber films with collagen hydrogel, and each layer was in a certain orientation. This model could induce cells' anisotropic arrangement, maturation and directional beating. As expected, the three layers of cardiac tissue with 0-45-90 degree orientation had a composition effect by each layer, resulting in the centripetal contraction, which was a similar function with the natural myocardium: shortening simultaneously in all directions. This 3d cardiac tissue exhibited a fast beating velocity of 7000 μm/s, at 1.5-2Hz. This model is also convenient for the future endotheliazation studies, by using sacrificial biomaterials and bio-printing between the layers. Read more...

Bone Defect Repair

1.5cm defect, 100% Regeneration

This is my bachelor graduation project in 2016-2017, also a part of a Marie Curie Project. This project was to repair 1.5cm bone defect of rabbit's radius bone, using 3D composite scaffolds (PLGA/n-HA nanofiber films wrapping PLGA anti-opal porous scaffolds) which are designed to mimic the different composition and regeneration rate of interior and exterior part of the bone. We achieved 40% bone regeneration 4 weeks postoperative, >80% at 8 weeks and 100% at 16 weeks, tested by multipical methods. Read more...
