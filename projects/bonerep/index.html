Bone Defect Repair

1.5cm Defect, 100% Regeneration

Overview

This is my bachelor graduation project in 2016-2017, also a part of a Marie Curie Project. This project was to repair 1.5cm bone defect of rabit's radius bone, using 3D composite scaffolds (PLGA/HA nanofiber films wrapping PLGA anti-opal porous scaffolds) which are designed to mimic the different composition and regeneration rate of interior and exterior part of the bone. We achieved 40% bone regeneration 4 weeks postoperative, >80% at 8 weeks and 100% at 16 weeks, tested by multipical methods.

Material & Structure design

Understanding the composition and structure of bone is a prerequisite for scaffold design. The basic components of bone are HA and collagen fibers, and the fiber diameter is about 200 nm. The overall structure can be mainly divided into exterior compact bone and interior cancellous bone or bone marrow. Those different parts have different regeneration rate. Considering the composition, structure and regeneration rate of long bones, the composite scaffolds prepared in this experiment have two aspects: material and structural composite, to simulate the composition and the internal & external structures of bone.

In this study, PLGA 3D porous scaffolds with a pore size >300 μm was used to obtain sufficient vascularization and to guide osteogenesis. Fiber membranes are also useful for efficient promotion of cell adhesion migration and for the principle of membrane-guided tissue regeneration. The inner side of the composite scaffold is designed as an inverse opal porous scaffold with a pore size of 400 μm to obtain sufficient vascularization and to guide osteogenesis; and a PLGA/HA nanofiber film with a fiber diameter of 200 nm is wrapped on the outer side to temporarily replace the bone matrix and guide bone formation. At the same time, in order to match the different growth speeds inside and outside the bone formation process, the inner side will use the faster degraded PLGA, and the outer side will be slower.
